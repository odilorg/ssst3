### **Section 2: 5 Proven Solutions When E-Visa Site Fails (900 words)**

**H2:** Solutions That Actually Work: Ranked from Free to Paid

**Solution #1: Browser Troubleshooting (Free - Try First) **
** Success Rate: 60% of problems **

** Steps:**
1. ** Clear browser cache and cookies **
   - Chrome: Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data
   - Select "All time" for time range
   - Check "Cookies" and "Cached images"

2. ** Try different browser **
   - Primary: Google Chrome (most compatible)
   - Secondary: Mozilla Firefox
   - Avoid: Safari (most reported issues)
   - Mobile: Desktop mode often works better than mobile app

3. ** Disable ad blockers / VPNs **
   - Ad blockers interfere with Stripe payment
   - VPNs can trigger security blocks
   - Disable temporarily during application

4. ** Incognito/Private window **
   - Prevents cache issues
   - Fresh session each time

5. ** Try at different times **
   - Early morning Uzbekistan time (3-6 AM) = less traffic
   - Avoid applying on weekends (higher volume)
   - Website maintenance typically Tuesday/Thursday nights

** When to move to Solution #2:** After 3-4 failed attempts with browser fixes

** Solution #2: Technical Workarounds (Free) **
** Success Rate: 25% of remaining problems **

** For Photo Issues:**
- Photo requirements: 3.5x4.5cm, white background, <1MB, JPEG
- Use: https://www.visa-photos.com/ or https://visafoto.com/uzbekistan
- These sites auto-resize and format correctly
- Take with phone, upload to site, download reformatted version

**For Payment Issues:**
- Try different card: Visa usually works better than Mastercard
- Call bank: "I'm making payment to Uzbekistan" to unblock international
- Some cards require 3D Secure enabled
- Use bank card instead of credit card
- Try smaller border countries' versions: kazkhstan.evisa.mfa.uz (sometimes more stable)

**For Citizenship Dropdown:**
- Refresh page (Ctrl+F5 hard refresh)
- Wait 30 seconds for full page load
- Click dropdown, wait, scroll using arrow keys instead of mouse
- If all fails, try from different device (phone vs computer)

### **Section 5: Mistakes to Avoid (400 words)**

**H2:** Uzbekistan E-Visa Mistakes: Don't Make These Common Errors

**Mistake #1: Booking Flights Before Getting Visa**
- Don't assume visa will be approved
- Wait for e-visa before booking non-refundable flights
- Exception: Use iVisa.com or visa service can be more confident

**Mistake #2: Wrong Photo Format**
- Photo must be: 3.5x4.5cm, white background, <1MB, JPEG
- Selfies not allowed
- Hat not allowed (unless religious, with proof)
- Glasses OK if no reflection
- Use visa photo service to ensure compliance

**Mistake #3: Name Doesn't Match Passport EXACTLY**
- "John Michael Smith" vs "John M. Smith" = rejection
- Include all middle names if in passport
- Special characters must match exactly
- Copy from passport, don't type from memory

**Mistake #4: Applying Too Late**
- Official site: Need 3 working days minimum
- But: Issues happen, need buffer
- Apply minimum 7 days before, ideally 2+ weeks
- iVisa.com: Can be faster (1-2 days)

**Mistake #5: Not Checking Spam Folder**
- Approval emails go to spam/junk frequently
- Gmail sometimes blocks @mfa.uz completely
- Check every 12 hours during processing
- Add @mfa.uz to contacts before applying

**Mistake #6: Wrong Arrival Date**
- 30-day visa starts from arrival date you enter
- Doesn't start when you actually arrive
- Can't change after issuance
- Be conservative: If uncertain, enter later date

**Mistake #7: Not Registering in Uzbekistan**
- Must register within 3 days
- Hotels do automatically (but verify!)
- Private accommodation: Must register at OVIR
- Keep registration slips (they check at departure)
- Fines: $2,000+ for violations

**Mistake #8: Using Damaged Passport**
- Passport must be in good condition
- No tears, water damage, loose pages
- Must have blank page for stamps
- 6+ months validity required

**Mistake #9: Skipping Travel Insurance**
- Not required for visa, but essential
- Uzbek healthcare inadequate
- Evacuation costs $50,000+
- Get policy covering Uzbekistan

**Mistake #10: Not Double-Checking Details**
- Review all entries before submitting
- Can't modify after submission
- Must contact embassy to cancel/reapply
- Takes additional time

**Q1: How long does Uzbekistan e-visa take?**
A: Official site: 3 working days. iVisa.com: 1-2 days. However, apply 7-10 days before travel for buffer.

A: Can't edit after submission. Must contact embassy to cancel ($50 fee) then reapply. Double-check everything!

**Q3: Can I get refund if rejected?**
A: Generally no. Payment is non-refundable. But rejections are rare if documents correct.

A: Yes, every passenger needs individual e-visa, including infants.

**Q5: Can I extend my 30-day visa?**
A: Not easily. Need to apply at OVIR in Tashkent, pay fee, provide reasons. Better to apply for correct duration initially.

**Q7: Is iVisa.com legitimate?**
A: Yes. Registered visa service. More expensive but reliable. 4.2/5 on TrustPilot. Many travelers use successfully.

**Q8: Can I apply from any country?**
A: Yes. Only requirement is internet access and email. No need to be in home country.

**Q9: What nationalities can get e-visa?**
