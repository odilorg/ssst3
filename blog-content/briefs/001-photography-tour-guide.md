### **Section 5: Camera Settings and Technical Details (500 words)**

**H2:** Camera Settings for Uzbekistan Photography

**This adds practical value, making post comprehensive guide**

**H3:** Registan Square Settings
- **Blue Hour (pre-dawn):** ISO 800-1600, f/4-f/8, 1-5 second exposure, tripod essential
- **Sunrise:** ISO 200-400, f/8-f/11, 1/60-1/250 sec
- **Details (tiles, patterns):** ISO 100-200, f/5.6-f/8, 1/125+ sec

**H3:** Desert Fortress Settings
- **Golden Hour on Adobe:** ISO 100-200, f/8-f/11, expose for highlights
- **Dune textures:** ISO 100, f/8, focus on patterns
- **Sunset silhouettes:** ISO 200, f/11, spot meter for sky

**H3:** Street Photography Settings
- **Markets:** Aperture Priority f/4-f/5.6, Auto ISO, continuous AF
- **Vendors:** Single-point AF on eyes, f/2.8-f/4 for background blur
- **Wide shots:** f/8 for depth

**H3:** Astrophotography Settings
- **Milky Way:** ISO 3200-6400, f/2.8-f/4 (widest lens aperture), 20-30 sec, manual focus on infinity
- **Star trails:** ISO 200, f/4-f/5.6, multiple 30-sec exposures stacked
- **Northern stars:** Point camera north, include foreground (fortress silhouette)

**H3:** Lens Recommendations
- **Wide (16-35mm):** Architecture, landscapes, astro
- **Mid (24-70mm):** Walkaround, street photography, details
- **Telephoto (70-200mm):** Portraits, compressing scenes, details from distance

**Note:** These settings are starting points, adjust based on conditions

**Q3: Can I join if I'm a beginning photographer?**
A: Absolutely! You'll learn more in 10 days than months of self-study. Guide provides personalized instruction.

**Q4: What if I don't have a professional camera?**
