## Common E-Visa Website Problems

Based on thousands of traveler reports from TripAdvisor, Reddit, and travel forums, here are the **8 most common problems** with the Uzbekistan e-visa website:

### 1. Payment Processing Failures (30% of issues)
This is the most frustrating problem. Your credit or debit card gets rejected repeatedly, often with vague error messages like "Payment failed - try again later." Common scenarios include:
- Card rejected even though it works for other purchases
- Payment page times out during processing
- No confirmation received after payment appears to go through
- Stripe integration errors or gateway timeouts

### 2. Photo Upload Failures (25% of issues)
The system has very specific requirements (3.5x4.5cm, white background, <1MB, JPEG format), but error messages are rarely helpful:
- Photo disappears after upload without error message
- "Invalid format" without explaining what's wrong
- Size restrictions unclear (must be under 1MB but dimensions also matter)
- Background not "white enough"
- Selfies rejected but system doesn't explain why

### 3. Citizenship Dropdown Freezes (20% of issues)
- Clicking the citizenship dropdown does nothing
- Dropdown menu doesn't populate with country list
- Selection can't be made, preventing form submission
- Problem occurs across multiple browsers (Chrome, Safari, Firefox)
- Mobile browsers particularly problematic

### 4. Timeout & Session Issues (15% of issues)
- Form times out while you're filling it out (often without warning)
- Session expires and you lose all entered data
- Must start completely over from beginning
- Especially frustrating for long or complex applications

### 5. Document Upload Problems (10% of issues)
- Passport scan rejected as "too blurry" or "too small"
- File type confusion (PDF sometimes rejected in favor of JPG only)
- Size restrictions unclear (how many MB is too much?)
- Passport must have 6+ months validity or system rejects at upload stage

### 6. Arrival Date Confusion (Very Common)
- Can't select dates less than 3 days from application date
- Confusion between "date of arrival" vs "planned arrival date" (30-day window starts from date you enter, not actual arrival)
- Can't modify dates after visa is issued
- Limited flexibility for changing travel plans

### 7. Email Delivery Failures
- Confirmation email never received (even after successful payment)
- Email goes to spam/junk folder
- Gmail sometimes completely blocks emails from @mfa.uz domain
- Processing takes 3 working days but expectation is immediate

### 8. "Under Processing" Status Forever
- Application stuck in "Under processing" status for 5+ days
- No way to contact support or check actual status
- Can't reapply because system says you have duplicate application
- No tracking number or reference provided

**How Common Are These Issues?**

Based on forum post volume and survey of travelers:
- **30-40% of applicants** face at least one technical problem
- **Peak season (March-May, September-November):** Issues increase 50%
- **Weekend applications:** Higher failure rates due to system load

You're definitely not alone if you're experiencing these frustrations.

## 5 Proven Solutions When E-Visa Site Fails

Here are the solutions ranked from free/do-it-yourself to paid/guaranteed options:

### Solution #1: Browser Troubleshooting (Free - Try First)

**Success Rate: 60% of technical problems**

Before spending money on alternative services, try these browser fixes:

**1. Clear Cache & Cookies**
- **Chrome:** Settings → Privacy and Security → Clear browsing data
- Select "All time" for time range
- Check both "Cookies" and "Cached images and files"
- Restart browser and try again

**2. Try Different Browsers**
- **Primary:** Google Chrome (most compatible with Uzbek government sites)
- **Secondary:** Mozilla Firefox
- **Avoid:** Safari (most reported compatibility issues with Uzbekistan e-visa site)
- **Mobile:** Desktop mode often works better than mobile browser app

**3. Disable Ad Blockers & VPNs**
- Ad blockers can interfere with Stripe payment processing
- VPNs can trigger security blocks (Uzbekistan sometimes blocks VPN traffic)
- **Temporarily disable both during application**

**4. Use Incognito/Private Window**
- Prevents cache-related issues
- Fresh session each attempt
- **Chrome:** Ctrl+Shift+N
- **Firefox:** Ctrl+Shift+P

**5. Try at Different Times**
- Early morning Uzbekistan time (3-6 AM) = less website traffic
- Avoid weekends (higher volume of applications)
- Website maintenance typically happens Tuesday/Thursday nights (avoid these times)

**When to Move to Solution #2:** After 3-4 failed attempts using these browser fixes.

### Step 3: Complete Application (Official Site)

If using official site, follow these steps precisely:

1. **Go to:** https://evisa.mfa.uz/
2. **Select language:** English (optional but recommended)
3. **Click:** "Apply Now"
4. **Fill personal details** (EXACTLY as they appear in passport):

   * First page only

   * Color scan preferred
8. **Enter arrival date:**
   * **CRITICAL: 30-day visa starts from this date**
   * Be conservative: If uncertain, enter later date
   * Can arrive AFTER this date but not before
9. **Enter accommodation details:**
   * Address of first hotel
   * Phone number (hotel or your mobile)
10. **Double-check everything**
11. **Submit application**

### Step 4: Payment

**Critical:** Have card ready and follow these steps:

**Before paying:**
- Clear browser cache (Ctrl+Shift+Delete)

**Official Site Timeline:**
- **Processing time:** Up to 3 working days (not including weekends)
- **Check spam folder:** Approval emails often go to spam
- **Add @mfa.uz to contacts:** Prevents future emails going to spam
- **Don't panic if takes 3 days:** This is normal

**iVisa.com Timeline:**
- **Processing time:** Usually 1-2 working days
- **Email delivery:** More reliable (less likely to go to spam)
- **Status updates:** You'll receive notifications along the way

**Status "Under processing":**
- **What it means:** Normal status, your application is in queue
- **When to worry:** If still "Under processing" after 4 working days
- **Action:** Contact support (call tourist police at 1173, they may help)

### Step 6: Receive E-Visa

**When Approved:**
- You'll receive email with PDF attachment
- **CRITICAL:** Check spam folder if not in inbox
- Email subject usually: "Uzbekistan E-Visa Approved" or similar
- PDF contains: Your e-visa with QR code, personal details, visa validity dates

**What to Do:**
1. **Download PDF** immediately
2. **Save backup:** Email to yourself, save to cloud (Google Drive, Dropbox)
3. **Print 2 copies:**
   * One in carry-on (to show at border)
   * One in checked luggage (backup)
   * Color print is better but black-and-white accepted
4. **Save digital copy on phone:** Screenshot or PDF in phone

**Verification Check:**
- Name matches passport exactly
- Passport number correct
- Dates are correct (30-day window from arrival date)
- QR code is clear (not blurry in print)

**If You Don't Receive E-Visa Approaching Travel Date:**
- Check spam folder (check ALL days since application)
- Check if any email filters moved it
- Contact: **Tourist Police Helpline: 1173** (English-speaking)
- Provide: Passport number, name, approximate application date

### Step 7: Register in Uzbekistan (After Arrival)

**This is CRITICAL and often overlooked!**

**Registration Requirement:**
- **Must register within 3 days** of arrival (including weekends)
- Applies to: All foreign visitors staying longer than 3 days
- **Your visa can be canceled and you can be deported if you don't register!**

**Hotels Register Automatically:**
- **99% of hotels/guesthouses register you**
- **BUT YOU MUST ASK FOR REGISTRATION SLIP**
- At check-in, say: "Please give me registration slip now"
- They may forget or delay
- Slip is small piece of paper with stamp

**What Registration Slip Looks Like:**
- Small paper (often A6/half-page)
- Hotel stamp
- Your name handwritten or printed
- Check-in/check-out dates
- Hotel manager signature

**Keep Registration Slip:**
- **DO NOT LOSE THIS**
- Immigration may check when you leave
- Keep safe with passport
- Take photo as backup

**If You Lose Registration Slip:**
- Ask hotel for copy (they should have records)
- If staying at multiple hotels, you need slip from each
- Keep them all until you depart Uzbekistan

**Private Accommodation (Airbnb, Rental, Friend's House):**
- **MUST register at OVIR** (Department of Foreign Travel)
- Go to local OVIR office within 3 days
- Bring: Passport, visa copy, landlord's documents
- Can be time-consuming process
- **Recommendation:** Stay in registered hotels instead

## Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: Booking Flights Before Getting Visa

**DON'T:** Book non-refundable flights before visa is approved

**WHY NOT:**
- Visa could be rejected (rare but possible)
- Technical issues could delay visa past travel date
- Cancellation/change fees expensive if visa delayed

**DO THIS INSTEAD:**
- **Book refundable or changeable flights**
- Or wait for visa approval before booking
- Check airline change policy
- Travel insurance covering visa issues (read fine print)

### Mistake #2: Wrong Photo Format

**Common Errors:**
- Selfies (not allowed)
- Photo too large (>1MB)
- Background not white
- Wearing hat (unless religious with proof)
- Glasses reflecting

**DO THIS INSTEAD:**
- **Use visa photo service:** https://www.visa-photos.com/ or https://visafoto.com/uzbekistan
- Professional photo is better than DIY
- Follow requirements exactly
- Get photo from photo booth that does passport photos

### Mistake #3: Name Mismatch

**Common Errors:**
- "John Michael Smith" vs "John M. Smith"
- Missing middle name that's in passport
- Spacing issues
- Special characters (accents, diacritics) missing

**DO THIS INSTEAD:**
- **Copy directly from passport** (don't type from memory)
- Include full middle name if in passport
- Match spacing and special characters exactly
- Double-check before submitting

### Mistake #4: Applying Too Late

**DON'T:** Apply less than 3 days before travel

**WHAT CAN HAPPEN:**
- Processing takes 3 working days minimum
- Technical issues add delay
- Crunch time creates stress
- May not receive visa in time

**DO THIS INSTEAD:**
- **Apply 2 weeks before travel** (safe buffer)
- 1 week minimum if using iVisa.com
- Account for weekends (processing doesn't happen Saturday/Sunday)

### Mistake #5: Not Checking Spam Folder

**WHAT HAPPENS:**
- Approval email goes to spam/junk
- Gmail sometimes blocks @mfa.uz completely
- You think visa never arrived
- Travel date approaches, you panic

**DO THIS INSTEAD:**
- **Check spam folder daily** during processing period
- Add @mfa.uz to your contacts BEFORE applying
- Search inbox for "Uzbekistan" "e-visa" "approved"
- Check email filters that may have moved messages

### Mistake #6: Wrong Arrival Date

**CRITICAL MISTAKE:**
- 30-day visa starts from arrival date you enter in application
- NOT from actual date you arrive
- Can't modify after submission
- If you arrive before stated date, visa is invalid

**DO THIS INSTEAD:**
- **Be conservative:** If uncertain of exact arrival, enter later date
- Can't arrive BEFORE stated date (but can arrive AFTER)
- Better to have extra days at end than invalid visa at start

### Mistake #7: Not Registering in Uzbekistan

**MISTAKE:**
- Not getting registration slip from hotel
- Losing registration slip
- Staying in private accommodation without registering

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- Cannot leave country (immigration checks at airport)
- Can be deported
- Fine of $2,000+ per week of unregistered stay

**DO THIS INSTEAD:**
- **Ask for registration slip at every hotel check-in**
- Keep all slips until you depart Uzbekistan
- Take photos of slips as backup
- If staying private, register at OVIR within 3 days

### Mistake #8: Using Damaged Passport

**REJECTION REASON:**
- Passport must be in good condition
- Tears, folds, water damage = rejected
- Must have blank page for stamps

**DO THIS INSTEAD:**
- Apply for new passport if damaged
- Ensure blank pages available
- Check 6+ months validity remaining

### Mistake #9: Skipping Travel Insurance

**MISTAKE:**
- No travel insurance
- Think "I'll be fine"

**WHY PROBLEM:**
- Uzbek healthcare inadequate for serious issues
- Medical evacuation costs $50,000+
- Embassy won't pay

**DO THIS INSTEAD:**
- **Get travel insurance with medical evacuation**
- Minimum $100,000 medical, $500,000 evacuation
- Companies: World Nomads, IMG Global, SafetyWing (research)
- Read policy - ensure Uzbekistan covered
- Declare all pre-existing conditions

## FAQ: Uzbekistan E-Visa

**Q: How long does Uzbekistan e-visa take to process?**

**A:** Official site: Up to 3 working days. iVisa.com: Usually 1-2 working days. However, apply 7-10 days before travel to account for potential technical delays.

**Q: What if I make a mistake on my visa application?**

**A:** You cannot edit after submission. You must contact the embassy to cancel ($50 fee) and then reapply. This is why we emphasize double-checking everything before submitting.

**Q: Can I get a refund if my visa application is rejected?**

**A:** Generally no. The payment is non-refundable even if rejected. However, rejections are rare if all documents are submitted correctly.

**Q: Do children need their own e-visa?**

**A:** Yes. Every passenger, including infants, needs an individual e-visa. There are no exceptions or combined family applications.

**Q: Can I extend my 30-day e-visa while in Uzbekistan?**

**A:** Not easily. You would need to apply at OVIR in Tashkent, pay an additional fee, and provide valid reasons. It's much better to apply for the correct duration initially. Most travelers find 30 days sufficient.

**Q: What if the e-visa website doesn't work at all?**

**A:** Use iVisa.com. It's a reliable commercial alternative that processes legitimate Uzbekistan e-visas. Yes, it costs more, but it works when the official site fails completely.

**Q: Is iVisa.com a legitimate service?**

**A:** Yes. They are a registered visa processing service. While more expensive than the official site ($35-70 vs $20-30), they are reliable and have helped thousands of travelers. They have 4.2/5 stars on TrustPilot.

**Q: Can I apply for Uzbekistan e-visa from any country?**

**A:** Yes. The only requirements are internet access and an email address. You don't need to be in your home country or even in the country where your passport was issued.

**Q: What nationalities are eligible for Uzbekistan e-visa?**

**A:** Most countries are eligible including US, UK, EU countries, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, and many others. Check the official site for the complete list: evisa.mfa.uz
