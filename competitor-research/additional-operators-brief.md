## 2. DOLORES TRAVEL GROUP

### Company Overview

**Years in operation:** Since 1998 (27 years!)
**Location:** Tashkent, Uzbekistan (headquarters) + Almaty, Kazakhstan office
**Market positioning:** Full-service DMC (Destination Management Company)
**Target audience:** International travelers, MICE, GCC countries
**Unique selling proposition:**
- Complete own infrastructure (vehicle fleet, guides, offices)
- Multilingual staff (8 languages)
- 25+ years experience
- MICE specialization
- Flexible pricing policy
- 24/7 support
- Debt-free company (financial stability)

### Tour Portfolio

**Pricing:** Very competitive
- 5 days/4 nights: From $690 USD per person
- 8 days/7 nights (Classic Silk Road): From $815 USD per person
- **Per day: $115-138/day** (CHEAPER than Advantour!)

**Categories:**
- Individual & Group Tours
- Historical & Cultural
- Adventure & Thematic
- Gastronomic Tours
- MICE services
- VIP & Incentive Tours

**Key Insight:** They offer **Gastronomic Tours** (but brief mentions only - not full culinary immersion)

### Strengths
1. **Complete infrastructure** - Own vehicles, guides, operations (control over quality)
2. **25+ years experience** (longer than Advantour!)
3. **Multilingual** (8 languages - broad market reach)
4. **MICE specialization** (corporate market)
5. **Very competitive pricing** ($115-138/day - budget positioning)
6. **24/7 support** (service standard)
7. **Debt-free** (financial reliability)

### Weaknesses (Opportunities for Us)

1. **Budget positioning** - Low prices = thin margins, perceived lower quality
2. **No specialist tours** - Focus on general cultural/historical only
3. **Generic tour descriptions** - Not storytelling-focused
4. **No small group emphasis** - Appears to cater to larger groups
5. **Limited online presence** - Basic website, minimal content marketing
6. **No festival tours** - Cultural calendar not leveraged
7. **No photography tours** - Opportunity gap
8. **No solo female focus** - Underserved market ignored

### Competitive Positioning

**Dolores vs Advantour:**
- DoloRES: $115-138/day (CHEAPER, budget)
- Advantour: $153-189/day (Mid-range)
- **Gap:** Advantour positioned as "better quality" at higher price

**Dolores vs Kalpak:**
- Dolores: $115-138/day (Budget)
- Kalpak: $258/day (Premium)
- **Gap:** Massive - Kalpak is 87-124% MORE expensive

**Dolores vs Us ($200-220/day):**
- Dolores: $115-138/day (Cost-conscious)
- **Us: $200-220/day (Mid-premium)**
- **Gap:** We position as "better experience" with boutique hotels, specialist guides, unique experiences

### Key Learning

**Dolores proves:** You CAN operate on $138/day, but:
- Likely using budget hotels
- Larger groups (economy of scale)
- Fewer inclusions
- More basic experiences

**Our advantage over Dolores:**
- Better hotels (boutique vs. budget)
- Smaller groups (12 vs. likely 20+)
- Specialist guides (photography, food, etc.)
- Unique experiences (not generic)
- Better storytelling/marketing
- Target affluent travelers who value quality over rock-bottom price

## 3. OREXCA

### Company Overview

**Years in operation:** Since 2003 (22 years)
**Market positioning:** Uzbekistan specialist, comprehensive portfolio
**Target audience:** Global (India, SE Asia, UK, USA, Italy, CIS countries mentioned)
**Unique selling proposition:**
- 100+ itineraries (extensive variety)
- Price range $40-3,800 (very broad)
- Small group sizes emphasized
- Multiple specialties: historical, adventure, gastronomic, helicopter, self-drive
- Large negative ion on UNESCO sites

### Tour Portfolio

**Number of tours:** 100+ itineraries (extensive!)
**Price range:** $40 day trip to $3,800 multi-country
**Core offering:** Extensive variety covering:
- Historical/cultural
- Adventure (jeep safaris)
- Gastronomic (brief mention)
- Helicopter tours
- Self-drive
- Religious tours
- UNESCO focus
- Remote destinations (Aral Sea, Darvaza gas crater)

### Key Features

**"Gastronomic Tours" mentioned but not detailed:**
- Same gap as others (opportunity for us)

**Small group sizes:**
- Emphasized but not specific numbers given

**"New friendships, sense of security"**
- Social aspect emphasized (good insight)

### Strengths
1. **Vast portfolio** (100+ tours - comprehensive)
2. **22 years experience** (proven track record)
3. **Global customer base** (multiple markets)
4. **Price range breadth** ($40-$3,800 serves all segments)
5. **Specialty access** (helicopters, remote destinations)
6. **Halal options** (Muslim market segment)
7. **UNESCO expertise** (heritage focus)

### Weaknesses (Opportunities for Us)

1. **Too many options** - Paradox of choice (easier to choose specialist operator)
2. **Not specialist ENOUGH** - "Jack of all trades, master of none"
3. **Gastronomic tours only mentioned** - Not full culinary immersion
4. **No photography tours** - Gap in market
5. **Generic descriptions** - Not storytelling-focused
6. **No festival tours** - Cultural calendar underutilized
7. **No solo female focus** - Underserved segment ignored

### Competitive Positioning

**Orexca vs Advantour:**
- Similar positioning (extensive portfolios, mid-range)
- Orexca: 100+ tours (more variety)
- Advantour: Better marketing presence
- Similar pricing ($153-189/day range)

**Orexca vs Kalpak:**
- Orexca: Broad, many options, mid-range
- Kalpak: Premium, focused, Swiss quality
- Different segments

**Orexca vs Us ($200-220/day):**
- Orexca: Generalist (100+ tours across all categories)
- **Us: Specialist (Uzbekistan ONLY + niche tours)**
- **Differentiation:** We own specific niches they don't serve deeply

### Key Learning

**Orexca proves:** You can have 100+ tours, but:
- May confuse customers with too many options
- Hard to be expert in everything
- Cross-selling harder than specialist

**Our strategy against Orexca:**
**"We don't do 100 tours, we do 15 tours - each one is a masterpiece"**

**Positioning:**
- Orexca: Walmart (everything, broad)
- Us: Specialty boutique (curated, expert)
