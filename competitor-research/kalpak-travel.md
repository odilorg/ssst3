## Detailed Tour Analysis: Best of Uzbekistan (11 days)

### Core Details
**Price:** From $2,840 USD per person
**Duration:** 11 days
**Group size:** Maximum 12 people (SMALL GROUP focus)
**Departure:** Guaranteed departure dates 2025-2026
**Route:** Tashkent → Nukus → Khiva → Bukhara → Samarkand → Tashkent

### Day-by-Day Itinerary

**Day 1: Arrival Tashkent**
- Arrive Tashkent, hotel check-in
- Rest after journey
- Welcome dinner (included)

**Day 2: Tashkent City Tour**
- Khast-Imam Complex (Quran of Caliph Uthman)
- Applied Art Museum
- Kukeldash Madrasah
- Chorsu Bazaar
- Uzbekistan Independence History
- Tour by Tashkent Metro (architectural stations)
- Overnight Tashkent

**Day 3: Tashkent → Nukus (Flight)**
- Morning flight to Nukus
- Check-in hotel
- Savitsky Museum tour (Igor Savitsky collection)
  - Over 90,000+ exhibits
  - World's 2nd largest Russian avant-garde collection after St. Petersburg
  - Karakalpak art and ancient artifacts
- Nukus Bazaar
- Overnight Nukus

**Day 4: Nukus → Khiva (Drive)**
- Drive via Khorezm desert ruins
- Option: Visit Mizdakhkan Necropolis (Sacred Cemetery)
- Arrive Khiva, check-in
- Free evening in Khiva

**Day 5: Khiva Full Day**
- UNESCO World Heritage Ichan-Kala (Old City)
- Kunya-Ark Fortress (17th century)
- Kalta Minor Minaret (uncompleted, iconic)
- Islam Khoja Minaret and Mosque (1910)
- Tash Hauli Palace (harem courtyard)
- Juma Mosque (218 wooden columns)
- Pakhlavan Mahmoud Mausoleum
- Free evening

**Day 6: Khiva → Bukhara (Drive)**
- Depart Khiva
- Drive through Kyzyl-Kum Desert (500 km, ~7-8 hours)
- En route: views of Amu Darya River
- Picnic lunch
- Arrive Bukhara, hotel check-in
- Free evening

**Day 7: Bukhara Full Day**
- Highlights: Samanid Mausoleum (9th century)
- Chashma Ayub (Job's Spring)
- Bolo Khauz Mosque
- Ark Fortress (5th-20th century fort)
- Zindan Prison
- Kalyan Minaret (47m tall, "Tower of Death")
- Ulugbek Madrasah
- Trading domes (Toki Sarrafon, Toki Telpak Furushon)
- Magoki Attori Mosque
- Lyab-i Hauz Ensemble (Nadir Divan-begi Khanqah)
- Free evening

**Day 8: Bukhara → Samarkand**
- Morning free in Bukhara
- Option: Additional time for shopping/souvenirs
- Drive OR train to Samarkand
- Arrive Samarkand, hotel check-in
- Free evening

**Day 9: Samarkand Full Day**
- **Registan Square** (crown jewel)
  - Ulugbek Madrasah (1417-1420)
  - Tillya Kari Madrasah (gold-covered interiors)
  - Sher-Dor Madrasah (tiger mosaic facade)
- Shakhi-Zinda Necropolis (living king, mausoleums)
- Gur-Emir Mausoleum (Tamerlane's tomb, Timurid dynasty)
- Rukhabad Mausoleum (legend of Saint's hair)
- Ulugbek Observatory (15th century, discovered 1,018 stars)

**Day 10: Samarkand → Tashkent**
- Siyob Bazaar (local market)
- Bibi Khanym Mosque (15th century, built by Tamerlane)
- Tomb of Saint Daniel
- Khovrenko Winery (since 1868) - wine tasting
- **Konigil village** (paper-making workshop)
  - Traditional mulberry paper (Bukhara paper)
  - Handmade process demonstration
- Continue to Tashkent (evening train)
- Farewell dinner

**Day 11: Departure from Tashkent**
- Airport transfer
- Tour ends

## Conclusion

Kalpak Travel is positioned as **premium small group specialist** with Swiss pedigree. Their $258/day pricing is 35-65% higher than Advantour, justified by smaller groups, more inclusions, and European brand trust.

**KEY GAP:** There's NO major competitor in **$200-220/day mid-premium segment** offering small groups with specialized experiences.

**Our opportunity:** Position between Advantour (budget-mid) and Kalpak (premium) at $200-220/day with specialist tours (photography, food, festivals) targeted at underserved niches.
