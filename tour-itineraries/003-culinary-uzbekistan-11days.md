**3:00 PM: UZBEK PLOV MASTERCLASS** (3 hours)

**Location:** Local chef's home kitchen or cooking school

**Why this is special:** Plov is Uzbekistan's national dish,每个家庭有自己的秘方。学习真正的技巧。

**Ingredients preparation (3:00-3:30 PM):**
- Wash and soak rice (special Uzbek rice)
- Slice onions thinly
- Julienne carrots properly (long, thin strips)
- Cut lamb into proper pieces
- Prepare garlic heads (whole, not peeled)
- Measure spices (cumin, barberries, salt)

**Cooking process (3:30-5:00 PM):**
- Heat kazan (cauldron) over fire
- Render lamb fat (or use vegetable oil)
- Brown lamb pieces (develop fond)
- Caramelize onions (critical step!)
- Add carrots (cook until softened)
- Add water, bring to boil
- Add rice in even layer (DO NOT STIR!)
- Add whole garlic heads on top
- Cover, steam 20-30 minutes
- **Most important:** Create "dam" (steam vent) with wooden spoon handle
- Rest 10 minutes off heat

**Each participant has own workstation** - actually cooking, not just watching

**Chef demonstrates:** Proper technique, then assists each person

**5:00-5:30 PM: Enjoy the plov you cooked**
- Gather around communal table
- Traditional blessing before eating
- Eat with hands (traditional way) or spoons
- Side dishes: fresh salad, pickles, chili sauce
- Your fresh tandoor bread from morning

## CULTURAL NOTES

**Food in Uzbek Culture:**
- Bread is sacred (cannot be placed upside down, cannot be wasted)
- Tea is offered to every guest (refusing is rude, but you can ask for small amount)
- Meals are communal (shared dishes)
- Elders served first
- Right hand traditionally used for eating (though spoons common now)
- Leaving small amount on plate shows you're satisfied (not hungry)
- Accommodations made for left-handed guests

**Hospitality:**
- Uzbek hospitality is legendary
- Guests treated with utmost respect
- Refusing food can be seen as rejecting hospitality
- **Our guides help navigate** if you're full or don't want something

**Tea Ceremony:**
- First cup: welcome
- Second cup: conversation
- Third cup: blessing
- Traditional to drink 3 cups minimum
- Green tea most common (kuk-choy)
- Black tea for special occasions
- Always served with snacks (dried fruits, nuts, candy)
