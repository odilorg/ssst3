# PRE-TOUR PREPARATION GUIDE
## How to Prepare for Your Uzbekistan Journey

---

## 2-3 MONTHS BEFORE DEPARTURE

### For ALL Tours:

**☐ Apply for Uzbekistan e-Visa**
- Go to: e-visa.gov.uz
- Cost: $20 USD
- Processing: 3 working days
- Print and bring with passport
- **Start now:** Processing can be delayed

**☐ Book International Flights**
- Arrive Tashkent Day 1 (anytime before 6 PM recommended)
- Depart Tashkent Day [X] (after 6 PM unless extending)
- Airport code: TAS (Tashkent International Airport)
- **Flight search tips:**
  * Turkish Airlines (via Istanbul - good connections)
  * Uzbekistan Airways (direct from some cities)
  * Aeroflot (via Moscow)
  * Look for flights 3-4 months before for best prices

**☐ Purchase Travel Insurance**
- **Required for:** Archaeology, Adventure tours
- **Recommended for:** All tours
- Coverage: Medical ($100k+), Evacuation ($250k+), Trip Cancellation
- Providers: World Nomads, IMG, Allianz
- Get "Cancel for Any Reason" if within 14 days of booking

**☐ Physical Preparation (Baseline for All Tours)**
- **Walking practice:** 30-45 minutes, 3-4x weekly
- **Break in hiking boots:** Wear them on walks!
- **Build stamina:** Gradually increase distance
- **Core strength:** Planks, side planks (helps with all tours)

**☐ Medical Preparations**
- **Check vaccinations:** Routine boosters recommended (hepatitis A/B, typhoid)
- **Visit doctor:** If you have health conditions, get clearance
- **Fill prescriptions:** Bring extras + copies of prescriptions
- **First aid kit:** Assemble (band-aids, pain relief, anti-diarrheal, any personal medications)
- **Copy medical documents:** Keep copies separate from originals

**☐ Download Useful Apps**
- **WhatsApp:** Primary communication method in Uzbekistan
- **Google Translate:** Download Uzbek offline language pack
- **Maps.me:** Download Uzbekistan offline maps
- **Google Maps:** Download offline areas for cities you'll visit
- **XE Currency:** Track exchange rates
- **Uber/Yandex.Go:** Taxis in Tashkent (not needed often - guides handle transport)

**☐ Learn Basic Uzbek Phrases**
- Salaam (Hello)
- Rahmat (Thank you)
 - Xayr (Goodbye)
- Ok (Yes/Okay)
- We provide full phrase sheet 2 weeks before departure

## **IF TAKING PHOTOGRAPHY TOUR:**

**☐ Camera Equipment Check**
- Clean camera sensor
- Check all lenses (functionality, cleanliness)
- Test all batteries (old batteries lose capacity)
- Order new batteries if needed
- Format memory cards
- Order extra memory cards (more than you think you need!)
- Clean lens filters
- Test tripod (make sure stable)
- Order/replace: Lens cleaning kit, rocket blower, microfiber cloths

**☐ Practice Before Trip**
- **Bracketing:** Learn exposure bracketing for high-contrast scenes
- **Manual mode:** Get comfortable with full manual control
- **Focusing:** Practice manual focus (essential for night photography)
- **Golden hour:** Shoot at sunrise/sunset to understand light
- **Night photography:** Practice shooting stars before you come (even from backyard)
- **Long exposures:** Neutral density filters, calculating exposures
- **Composition:** Review rule of thirds, leading lines
- **Portrait techniques:** Practice with people (permission, connection, natural poses)

**☐ Complete Pre-Tour Photography Assignment**
- We send this 3 weeks before departure
- Typically: Submit 5-10 of your best photos for review
- Guide provides feedback before tour
- Helps guide understand your level and goals

**☐ Download Photo Processing Software**
- **Lightroom:** Subscribe to Adobe Photography plan ($10/month)
- **Alternative:** Luminar, Capture One, or free (Darktable)
- Learn basics before tour (import, cull, basic adjustments)
- Practice on your photos
- Bring laptop capable of running software smoothly

**☐ Gear List Double-Check**
- Wide-angle lens (16-35mm)
- Mid-range zoom (24-70mm)
- Telephoto (70-200mm)
- Tripod (ESSENTIAL)
- Extra batteries (minimum 4)
- Memory cards (minimum 100GB total)
- Circular polarizing filter
- ND filter (for long exposures)
- Lens cleaning kit
- Laptop for processing
- External hard drive/SSD (backup!)
- Headlamp (night photography)
- Camera rain cover (desert dust)

## **IF TAKING ARCHAEOLOGY TOUR:**

**☐ SERIOUS Physical Training**
- **Target:** Hike 10-12 km on uneven terrain
- **Frequency:** 3-4x weekly
- **Terrain:** Find trails, hills, uneven ground (not pavement)
- **Duration:** Build up to 2-3 hour continuous hiking
- **Backpack:** Practice with 5-8 kg daypack
- **Stairs:** Lots of stair climbing (fortresses have stairs!)
- **Core:** Planks, side planks (protect back)
- **Ankle stability:** Balance exercises
- **Start 3 months before:** This is serious physical preparation!

**☐ Medical Check-Up**
- Visit doctor for physical
- Discuss your travel plans
- Get medical clearance
- Address any health concerns
- Fill prescriptions
- Update vaccinations

**☐ Boots - CRITICAL**
- **Must be:** Sturdy hiking boots with ankle support
- **Break them in NOW:** Wear on every walk/hike
- **Test on uneven terrain:** Make sure stable
- **Waterproof:** Treat with waterproofing spray
- **Check soles:** Good tread for sand/rock
- **Test with your hiking socks:** Ensure good fit
- **Bring 2 pairs of laces:** Spare in case one breaks
- **Don't skimp:** Good boots prevent sprains, blisters, injuries

**☐ Gear Acquisition (Start Early)**
- Daypack (25-30L) - test with weight
- Trekking poles (optional but recommended)
- High SPF sunscreen
- Wide-brim hat (essential!)
- Sunglasses (UV protection, wraparound)
- Headlamp (plus extra batteries)
- Water bottles (total 3-4 liter capacity)
- Blister care (moleskin, Compeed, sports tape)
- Gaiters (optional - keeps sand out of boots)

**☐ Practice Hiking in Heat**
- If possible, hike in warm weather
- Learn your heat tolerance
- Practice hydration strategies
- Know signs of heat exhaustion (in yourself)

**☐ Reading on Central Asian Archaeology**
- Sergey Tolstov "Ancient Khorezm" (classic but academic)
- Articles on Khorezm civilization
- Silk Road history
- Zoroastrianism basics
- Buddhism in Central Asia
- Not required but enhances experience!

**☐ Download Offline Content**
- Maps of Uzbekistan (detailed, topographic if possible)
- Archaeology apps (if any)
- Kindle books on region
- Star chart apps (for stargazing)

## **IF TAKING ADVENTURE TOUR:**

**☐ SERIOUS Physical Training - START EARLY (3 months!)**
- **This is most physically demanding tour!**
- **Target fitness:**
  * Hike 10-15 km with 8-10 kg backpack
  * Continuous activity 5-7 hours
  * Handle heat (90-100°F)
  * Sand dune climbing (very strenuous!)
  * Camel riding (uses core muscles)
  * Camping (sleeping on ground, basic facilities)

**Training Schedule:**
- **Months 1-2:** 45-60 min walks/hikes 4x weekly
- **Month 3:** Long hike 10-12 km 1x weekly + shorter hikes 3x weekly
- **Add weight:** Gradually add weight to backpack
- **Terrain:** Trail hiking essential (uneven ground)
- **Hills:** Add elevation gain
- **Core:** Planks, side planks (daily)
- **Legs:** Squats, lunges (2-3x weekly)
- **Cardio:** Swimming, cycling, running (cross-training)

**☐ Medical Check - ESSENTIAL**
- **Required:** Doctor's clearance
- Discuss: Heart, lungs, blood pressure
- Medications for trip
- Any conditions that could be problematic in desert
- Be honest about fitness level

**☐ Boots - CRITICAL!**
- **See archaeology section above**
- **Same requirements + MUST handle sand**
- Gaiters highly recommended (keeps sand out)
- Break in thoroughly (3 months of wearing!)
- Test on hot hikes

**☐ Gear - MANDATORY ITEMS**
- **See detailed adventure tour gear list**
- **DO NOT SKIP ITEMS - you'll regret it!**
- Start acquiring NOW (order online if needed)
- Test everything before trip
- Practice packing backpack efficiently

**Essential items to prioritize:**
- Quality hiking boots
- Daypack (comfortable with weight)
- Headlamp (hands-free essential)
- Water system (3-4 liter capacity)
- Sun protection (hat, sunglasses, SPF, lip balm)
- Blister prevention (moleskin, sports tape)
- Wet wipes (NO showers for 2-3 nights!)
- Power bank (charge when you can)
- Warm layer (desert nights cold!)

**☐ Practice Desert Conditions (If Possible)**
- Hike during hottest part of day
- Test heat tolerance
- Practice hydration schedule
- Know your limits
- Learn signs of heat exhaustion

**☐ Mental Preparation**
- **Expect discomfort:** Camping is not luxury
- **Embrace challenges:** Sand, heat, basic facilities
- **Flexibility:** Desert travel requires adapting to conditions
- **Positive attitude:** Challenges make accomplishment meaningful!
- **Prepare for:** No Wi-Fi, limited electricity, basic bathrooms, sleeping on ground

**☐ Reading on Aral Sea Disaster**
- The Aral Sea environmental crisis
- Karakalpak culture
- Desert survival stories
- Expedition narratives

**☐ Physical Documents**
- **Fitness waiver:** We'll send to sign
- **Medical form:** Complete honestly
- **Emergency contact:** Multiple contacts
- **Insurance:** Must provide proof of adventure travel coverage

## DEPARTURE DAY

**☐ Wake Up Early**
- Give yourself plenty of time
- No rushing = less stress

**☐ Final Document Check**
- Passport (in hand before you leave house!)
- Flight confirmation
- Phone
- Wallet
- Everything else can be replaced

**☐ Hydrate**
- Drink water before flight
- Bring empty water bottle through security, fill on other side

**☐ Travel to Airport**
- Arrive 3 hours before international flight (recommended)
- Have passport ready
- Have flight confirmation ready
- Keep valuables with you

**☐ At Airport**
- Check in
- Get boarding pass
- Check luggage (if you have checked bag)
- Keep carry-on with you
- Go through security
- Find gate
- Charge devices if time

**☐ On Flight**
- Set watch to Uzbekistan time (start adjusting mindset)
- Try to sleep if arriving morning
- Stay hydrated (water, not alcohol)
- Get up and walk every few hours (prevents clots)
- Charge devices if seat has USB
- Enjoy the journey!

**☐ Upon Arrival in Tashkent**
- Follow signs to Immigration
- Have passport and visa ready
- Fill out arrival card (on plane or at airport)
- Go through passport control
- Collect luggage
- Go through customs (usually just walk through green channel)
- **Look for our driver:**
  * They'll be in arrivals hall with sign: "JAHONGIR TRAVEL - [Your Name]"
  * If you don't see them immediately, they're likely there - look carefully
  * If still can't find: Call/WhatsApp guide (number provided in pre-tour packet)
  * Or show our contact info to information desk
- Driver takes you to hotel
- Check in
- Rest!
- Meet guide at scheduled time (usually next morning, but could be same day)

**☐ You've arrived in Uzbekistan!**
- Adventure begins now
- Be open, be curious, be respectful
- Ask questions
- Try new things
- Take photos (but also be present)
- Connect with people
- Learn and grow
- HAVE INCREDIBLE EXPERIENCE!
